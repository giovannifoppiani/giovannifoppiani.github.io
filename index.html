Giovanni Foppiani

Communication designer & PhD researcher

About

Through his design practice, he collaborates with eco-social conscious brands, public institution, and associations to strengthen their communication potential.

By investigating the mutual relationships between ecology, technology, and social ecosystems, his research leads to scenarios that frame new ways of conceiving interspecies cooperation and collaborations.

His approach combines critical design thinking with hands-on experimentation, creating bridges between academic research and practical applications. This methodology allows for the development of communication strategies that are both theoretically grounded and immediately applicable to real-world challenges.

Giovanni's work has been recognized internationally, with projects featured in major design exhibitions and publications. He continues to explore the intersection of design, technology, and ecology, seeking innovative ways to address pressing environmental and social issues.

Bio

Giovanni Foppiani is a communication designer and PhD researcher at LUT and RMIT University, whose work explores techno-social ecosystems for the common good. With a master's degree in Communication Design from ISIA Firenze, his practice focuses on digital sustainability, social innovation, and regenerative mobility. He has served as an assistant professor and researcher at Università Iuav di Venezia and developed a sustainable critical platform with the collective Criticity. His collaborations with public institutions and organizations like Rifo-lab and Oxfam Italy reflect a consistent commitment to Communication for the Common Good.
